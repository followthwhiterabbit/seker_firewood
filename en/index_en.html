Why Should You Use Firewood?

In recent years, due to rising costs and sharp price increases in other energy sources, heating with wood has become a necessity for many of us.
As a fuel source, wood is a natural form of heating that requires no subscription. It comes in various types such as oak, beech, and hornbeam, offering multiple alternatives. It is a type of fuel that does not cause nature- or human-induced disruptions, is environmentally friendly, and has roots as old as humanity itself.

As you may know, the taste of food cooked over a wood fire, bread baked in a wood oven, or tea brewed on embers is something many of us still remember fondly. The health benefits it provides are also an undeniable reality.

With developing technology, fireplace systems, stoves, and furnaces that retain heat inside help save fuel and extend heat energy over longer periods.

This results in economic savings for both companies and households that use it for heating, easing the burden on budgets.

What Processes Do the Firewoods Go Through Before Reaching You?

The felling of trees from our lifeblood—the forests—is subject to specific permissions and is only allowed in designated areas authorized by the Ministry of Forestry.
Since its establishment, our company has made it a mission to obtain all necessary permits from the Ministry and to act with the utmost care for the survival and sustainability of forests. Our principle, “Keep nature alive so that humans may live,” has been adopted as a core philosophy and instilled in all our staff.

Our operations are carried out in accordance with the Ministry of Forestry's reforestation and regeneration projects, and all our personnel undergo the necessary training stages.

Let us not forget: once we consciously decide not to throw even a single piece of litter on the ground, our environment will start to look much cleaner.

This also results in economic savings for both companies and households that prefer it as a heating source, easing the burden on budgets.
